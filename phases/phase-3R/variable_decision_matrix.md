# v1 Variable Decision Matrix

**Purpose:** Document the v1 similarity variable set as deviations from the Nebraska Department of Education's reference methodology. Each row records a Nebraska variable and the v1 disposition (Include, Exclude, Substitute), with reasoning concentrated on the deviations.

**Status:** Locked for v1 commit, with Path C consolidation applied per the redundancy audit. Empirical confirmations (homelessness availability, expulsion variability, redundancy structure, K stability, achievement sensitivity) are reported in `methodology_inspection.md`.

**Reference:** Nebraska Department of Education. *Methodology to Compare Districts and Schools: A Technical Report.* January 18, 2019. Variable set drawn from Table 1, p. 5.

## v1 Variable Count

After Path C consolidation: 17 variables in total. Per-level used count is 16 for Elementary, Middle, and Other level groups, and 17 for High School (graduation rate adds at HS, restricted per Nebraska's own treatment). The set comprises 15 variables drawn from Nebraska's set (9 NDE-sourced demographic and structural variables; 6 Census ACS community variables) and 2 additions beyond Nebraska (SPED percentage from Texas's seven-variable approach, and district-level average teacher salary as the v1 finance variable).

Three Nebraska finance variables are substituted (rows 16-18). Nine Nebraska variables are excluded:

- Three achievement variables (ELA, Math, Science proficiency) on methodological grounds: matching on achievement systematically attenuates the academic flag toward zero; aligned with Texas methodology and empirically tested in cohort-level Jaccard sensitivity (mean 0.385).
- Two discipline variables (suspensions, expulsions) on policy-variation and low-variability grounds: WA discipline policies vary across districts in ways that make matching partly match on policy implementation rather than structural similarity, and Nebraska themselves drop expulsions for low variability.
- Teacher master's degree percentage on data-availability grounds: no school-level credential data is queryable on data.wa.gov; v2 path identified parsing S-275 raw personnel extracts.
- Three Census ACS community variables (per capita income, bachelor's degree percentage of population aged 25 and over, total population) consolidated out after the redundancy audit: per capita income and bachelor's degree percentage are redundant with median household income (Cluster 1, r ≥ 0.77 with median_household_income, r = 0.93 between the two dropped variables); total population is redundant with population_density (Cluster 2, r = 0.85 to 0.91 across levels).

This is a leaner Nebraska adaptation rather than a Texas-style minimal set. The brief is canonical for methodology decisions; this matrix is the path lookup that shows variable-by-variable disposition and current schema location.
